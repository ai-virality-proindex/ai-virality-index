# Case Study #3: DeepSeek's Hype Collapse — When Promises Don't Deliver

## The Event
DeepSeek entered 2026 on a wave of hype from its January V3 moment. The market expected DeepSeek V4 — a trillion-parameter multimodal model — to launch in early March 2026, timed for China's annual "Two Sessions" parliamentary meetings (March 4).

But V4 never shipped. As mid-February, late-February, and early-March windows all passed without a release, the hype evaporated. Meanwhile, Anthropic publicly accused DeepSeek of industrial-scale distillation of Claude's capabilities (16M exchanges via 24,000 fraudulent accounts), further damaging the brand.

## What AVI Showed

### The Decline in Numbers
| Metric | Week 1 (Feb 16-22) | Week 4 (Mar 9-15) | Change |
|--------|--------------------|--------------------|--------|
| Trading Score | 40.7 avg | 31.3 avg | **-23%** |
| Content Score | ~45 avg | ~27 avg | **-39%** |
| Trading Rank | #3 | #4 | -1 rank |

**Content -39% was the single largest decline of any model in any mode.**

### Component-Level Autopsy
| Component | Early Feb | Mid-March | What Happened |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| S (Social) | 67 | 6 | YouTube/HN buzz evaporated completely |
| T (Trends) | 36 | 12 | Google search interest cratered |
| N (News) | 50 | 25 | News shifted from "DeepSeek launching V4" to "DeepSeek accused of distillation" |
| G (GitHub) | 85 | 60 | GitHub remained relatively strong (open-source community loyal) |
| D (DevAdoption) | 68 | 27 | SDK downloads declining |
| M (Mindshare) | 90 | 40 | Wikipedia interest halved |

### Key Insight: GitHub Held While Everything Else Collapsed
DeepSeek's GitHub score (G) remained the strongest of any model throughout the period (50-85 range), even as all other components cratered. This reveals a crucial dynamic:

**Open-source developer loyalty persists through hype cycles.**

DeepSeek's core user base (AI researchers, open-source developers) continued starring and forking repos even as mainstream attention vanished. G was the "floor" preventing a complete collapse.

### Momentum Analysis
- Only **3 positive delta7 days out of 22** in content mode — the most persistently negative momentum of any model
- Delta7 averaged **-2.60** in trading mode — steady, relentless decline

## What a Trader Would Have Done
1. **Feb 23** (Anthropic distillation accusation): Short signal — news turns negative, N-component spike is bearish for DeepSeek
2. **Mar 4** (V4 expected but not shipped): Confirm short — failed catalyst, no reason for bounce
3. **Mar 10+**: Look for signs of G (GitHub) stabilizing as potential floor — possible long entry for contrarian play if/when V4 eventually ships

## Key Takeaway
> **AVI captured the anatomy of a hype collapse in real-time.** The component breakdown revealed that DeepSeek's decline wasn't uniform — Social and Trends collapsed first (fickle mainstream attention), while GitHub held firm (loyal developer base). This pattern is a template: when S and T collapse but G holds, the decline is likely to stabilize. When G also breaks, it's a terminal decline.

### The "Hype Without Delivery" Pattern
```
Phase 1: High expectations → elevated S, T, N, M
Phase 2: Missed deadline → S drops first (social loses interest)
Phase 3: Negative news → N shifts bearish, T follows
Phase 4: Stabilization → G provides floor (if open-source)
Phase 5: Recovery requires NEW catalyst (product launch)
```

This 5-phase pattern is now documented and can be applied to future missed launches.
